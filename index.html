Privacy Policy.

               

Last Updated: April 2026

               
                   

At Winpath Diagnostics, we recognize that health information is the most sensitive form of personal data. As an ISO 15189:2022 certified laboratory operating in Bengaluru, Karnataka, we are strictly committed to data integrity, customer confidentiality, and absolute transparency.

                   

This Privacy Policy outlines our practices in accordance with the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDPA), the Information Technology (Reasonable Security Practices and Procedures and Sensitive Personal Data or Information) Rules, 2011 (SPDI Rules) under the IT Act, 2000, and general principles of the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) regarding the safeguarding of protected health information (PHI).

                   

1. Information We Collect

                   

To provide accurate diagnostic services, we collect both Personal Information and Sensitive Personal Data or Information (SPDI) as defined under Indian law. This includes:

                   
                           
  • Demographic Information: Full name, age, gender, date of birth.
    •                        
  • Contact Information: Mobile number, email address, residential address (for home collection), and GPS coordinates (if explicitly provided via our digital platform).
    •                        
  • Sensitive Health Information (SPDI): Medical history, referring physician details, physiological conditions, clinical symptoms, and the resulting diagnostic pathology reports generated by our laboratory.
    •                    
                   

2. How We Use Your Information

                   

Your data is collected on the basis of legitimate operational consent and is utilized exclusively for the following purposes:

                   
                           
  • Clinical Processing: To register, process, and analyze your biological samples accurately.
    •                        
  • Report Delivery: To securely transmit your diagnostic results to you or your designated referring physician via email, WhatsApp, or secure portals.
    •                        
  • Quality Assurance: For internal clinical audits, quality control verifications required by ISO 15189:2022, and laboratory recalibration (in an anonymized format).
    •                        
  • Legal Compliance: To comply with mandatory reporting requirements mandated by the Indian Council of Medical Research (ICMR), the National Medical Commission (NMC), or local municipal health authorities (e.g., BBMP) during public health emergencies.
    •                    
                   

3. Data Sharing & Disclosure

                   

Winpath Diagnostics does not sell, rent, or lease your health data to any third-party marketing or pharmaceutical entities. Your information is only shared under strict confidentiality agreements with:

                   
                           
  • Your explicitly authorized referring medical practitioner.
    •                        
  • Secure, compliance-certified cloud infrastructure providers hosting our Laboratory Information Management System (LIMS).
    •                        
  • Government health regulatory bodies when legally bound by Indian law.
    •                    
                   

4. Data Security & HIPAA Benchmarks

                   

While operating within Indian jurisdiction, Winpath Diagnostics benchmarks its operational security against global standards like HIPAA to ensure maximum protection. We deploy end-to-end encryption for data transmission, role-based access control (RBAC) within our laboratory, and secure archival systems. Biological samples are barcoded to minimize human identifiable exposure during the testing lifecycle.

                   

5. Data Retention & Patient Rights

                   

As per the Clinical Establishments (Registration and Regulation) Act, 2010, diagnostic laboratories are required to maintain patient records for specific statutory durations. Upon expiration of this mandatory period, records are securely archived or purged.

                   

Under the DPDP Act, 2023, you hold the Right to Access your records and the Right to Correction of demographic inaccuracies. While you possess the right to withdraw consent for future communications, statutory medical records cannot be erased upon request due to prevailing clinical laws.
